Movie Sales

In this project, I used Python in Jupyter Notebook to analyze a movie dataset and explore factors highly correlated with box office gross. My initial hypothesis was that gross revenue would be strongly correlated with both budget and the prominence of certain movie studios. After conducting data cleaning, visualization, and correlation analysis using libraries such as Pandas, Matplotlib, and Seaborn, I discovered that gross revenue did indeed have a strong positive correlation with budget, confirming part of my hypothesis. However, rather than movie studios, I found that the second highest correlation was with critic votes, indicating that critical reception might be a more significant factor than company reputation in predicting box office success. This insight provided a detailed understanding of the variables that impact movie revenue.

Bike Sales

In this project, I used Excel to create an interactive dashboard analyzing a bike sales dataset to identify factors influencing customers' decisions to purchase a bike. I built the dashboard using pivot tables and charts, which included a bar chart showing average income by gender, a line chart displaying customer commute distances, and another line chart depicting customer age ranges, with all graphs segmented by whether the customer purchased a bike. To enhance interactivity, I added slicers for region, marital status, number of cars owned, and occupation, allowing users to filter the data dynamically across all three graphs. This setup provided a clear, interactive view of the customer demographics and lifestyle factors related to bike purchases, helping to identify key trends and patterns.

Airbnb Sales

In this project, I used Tableau Public to create an interactive dashboard that offers a comprehensive view of key metrics for Airbnb listings across the city of Seattle. It includes the average price by zip code, supplemented with a map to visualize geographic pricing trends, as well as the average price and total number of listings based on bedroom count. Additionally, I have also included a seasonal revenue chart that highlights peak and off-peak periods throughout the year. Together, these visualizations provide valuable insights into pricing, property types, and seasonal demand, supporting data-driven decision-making for those looking to list properties on the site.
